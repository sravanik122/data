Ingredients:
1.5 cups basmati rice

1 cup fresh or frozen green peas (mutter)

2 tbsp oil or ghee

1 tsp cumin seeds

1 bay leaf

1-inch cinnamon stick

2-3 cloves

1 green chili, slit (optional)

1 medium onion, finely chopped

1 tsp ginger-garlic paste

1/2 tsp turmeric powder

1 tsp red chili powder (optional)

Salt to taste

3 cups water

Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:
Wash and soak rice:
Rinse basmati rice well and soak for 20-30 minutes. Drain and set aside.

Sauté spices and aromatics:
Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot or pressure cooker.
Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon, and cloves. Let them sizzle for a few seconds.
Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
Add ginger-garlic paste and green chili; sauté until raw smell disappears.

Add peas and spices:
Add green peas, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.

Add rice and water:
Add drained rice to the pot and gently mix with peas and spices.
Pour water and stir gently.

Cook rice:

On stovetop: Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and cook until rice is tender and water is absorbed (about 15-20 minutes).

Pressure cooker: Cook for 2 whistles on medium heat, then let pressure release naturally.

Rest and garnish:
Let the rice rest covered for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork.
Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
