Ingredients:
250 grams paneer (cottage cheese), cut into cubes

2 large onions, finely sliced

2 large tomatoes, pureed

1/4 cup cashew nuts (soaked in warm water for 15 minutes)

1/4 cup fresh cream or thick yogurt

2 tbsp ghee or oil

1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste

2-3 green cardamoms

1-inch cinnamon stick

3-4 cloves

1 bay leaf

1 tsp cumin seeds

1 tsp coriander powder

1 tsp garam masala powder

1/2 tsp turmeric powder

1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)

Salt to taste

Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

2 tbsp milk (to soak saffron strands, optional)

A few saffron strands (optional)

Instructions:
Prepare cashew paste:
Drain soaked cashews and grind into a smooth paste with a little water. Set aside.

Fry paneer:
Lightly shallow fry paneer cubes in a little ghee or oil until golden. Set aside. (Optional — you can skip this step for softer paneer.)

Make the gravy base:
Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Add bay leaf, cardamoms, cinnamon, cloves, and cumin seeds. Sauté till fragrant.
Add sliced onions and cook till golden brown.
Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté till raw smell disappears.

Add tomato puree:
Add tomato puree and cook till oil separates from the masala.

Add spices:
Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.

Add cashew paste and cream:
Stir in cashew paste and cook for 3-4 minutes on low heat.
Add fresh cream (or yogurt) and mix well. Cook for another 5 minutes.

Add paneer:
Add fried paneer cubes gently and coat them with the gravy.

Add garam masala and saffron:
Sprinkle garam masala powder.
If using saffron, soak strands in warm milk and add to the gravy for extra richness and aroma.

Simmer:
Cook on low heat for 5-7 minutes. Adjust consistency by adding a little water or milk if needed.

Garnish and serve:
Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
