Ingredients:
For the Koftas:
2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed

1 cup paneer (cottage cheese), grated

2 tbsp all-purpose flour (maida) or cornflour (for binding)

2 tbsp raisins

2 tbsp chopped cashew nuts

1 tsp garam masala

Salt to taste

Oil for frying

For the Gravy:
2 large onions, finely chopped or pureed

2 large tomatoes, pureed

1/4 cup cashew nuts (soaked in warm water and ground to paste)

1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste

1/2 cup fresh cream or thick yogurt

2 tbsp ghee or oil

1 tsp cumin seeds

1 tsp turmeric powder

1 tsp red chili powder

1 tsp coriander powder

1 tsp garam masala powder

Salt to taste

Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)

Instructions:
Prepare Koftas:
In a bowl, mix mashed potatoes, grated paneer, flour, garam masala, and salt.

Stuff each small ball of this mixture with a few raisins and chopped cashews. Shape into smooth balls.

Heat oil in a deep pan and shallow fry the koftas until golden brown. Drain on paper towels and set aside.

Prepare Gravy:
Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.

Add onions and saut√© until golden brown.

Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.

Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates from the masala.

Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.

Add cashew paste and cook for 3-4 minutes on low heat.

Stir in cream or yogurt and cook for another 5 minutes, adjusting the consistency with water if needed.

Sprinkle garam masala powder and mix gently.

Serve:
Add koftas to the gravy just before serving or serve them separately on the side (to keep them crisp).

Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
